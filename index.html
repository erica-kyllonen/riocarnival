CARNIVAL

Carnival in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, is a world-famous festival held in the days leading up to Ash Wednesday, marking the beginning of Lent. Known as “The Greatest Show on Earth,” Carnival features vibrant samba parades at the iconic Sambodromo, a stadium that hosts over 80,000 spectators. Samba schools spend months preparing their routines, costumes, and floats, competing in front of a panel of judges. The celebration is filled with the rhythms of drums and the colorful energy of Brazil’s Afro-European culture, with street parties called “blocos” bringing music and dancing to every corner of the city. These events range from family-friendly gatherings to lively, more daring celebrations. Beyond the parades, Carnival is a major economic boon for Rio, attracting millions of visitors and boosting tourism. The festivities extend into Rio’s breathtaking landscape, where visitors can explore famous attractions like Sugarloaf Mountain, Christ the Redeemer, and beaches like Copacabana and Ipanema. The city transforms into a week-long celebration filled with music, dance, and unforgettable cultural experiences, making Carnival a true embodiment of Brazilian spirit and joy.

The Sambadrome Parade
At the heart of the celebration is the Sambodromo, a massive stadium that accommodates over 80,000 spectators. Constructed in 1984, this venue allows samba schools to showcase elaborate performances. These schools prepare for months, perfecting their dance routines, creating dazzling costumes, and designing impressive floats. Each school is judged on various criteria, such as choreography, music, and the creativity of their costumes, with the competition culminating in the announcement of the winner on Ash Wednesday.
Carnival Street Parties
Rio’s neighborhoods celebrate the carnival parades, with cordoes, or samba-led groups of people, accompanied by percussionists, drummers and other musicians. Dancing, singing, excitement and party spirit- you could find all that and more on the streets of Rio, until the Carnival turned into a competitive arena for various blocos, or street parades. Today, participants are extravagantly dressed in their bright costumes as they strive to become the champions of the carnival.
